INSTRUCTIONS

  • Your internship officially begins on the start date mentioned in your Offer Letter email. You should start completing your tasks only after your internship has begun. Focus solely on the tasks within your designated domain as specified in your Offer Letter.

  • To be eligible for the Completion Certificate, you must complete at least two tasks in your internship track. The internship is self-paced, so you can work according to your own schedule.

  • You have the freedom to select your own learning resources to study and complete the tasks. Note that no formal training will be provided during the internship. Utilize online tutorials, courses, and other educational materials that best suit your learning style.

  • You are required to maintain a public GitHub repository for all the tasks you complete. Use the following naming convention for your repositories: "FutureIntern_TaskNumber". For example, if you are a Web Development intern working on your second task, name your repository "FutureIntern_WD_02".

  • After completing each task, you must create a LinkedIn post outlining the task you have completed and the knowledge you gained. Optionally, you can include a video in your post. Mention Future Intern in your post and use the specified hashtags to ensure visibility.

  • You will receive the Task Submission Form via email around the middle of your internship month. Use this form to submit links to your completed tasks. Ensure that your submissions are thorough and accurately reflect your work.

  • You are free to choose any programming language or framework to complete your tasks. For Data Science interns, tools like MS Excel, Power BI, Tableau, and Google Sheets are recommended.

  • Throughout your internship, we will be in constant contact with you to provide guidance and support. If you encounter any problems or have questions, do not hesitate to reach out to us. We are here to help you succeed.
